Quick answer

The Withings Body+ is the best smart scale for most people, offering accurate readings and seamless app integration.

Garmin fans should consider the Index S2, while budget buyers will appreciate the Eufy Smart Scale P1.

Smart scales have evolved far beyond simple weight trackers. They now measure body fat percentage, muscle mass, bone density, and even vascular health, giving you a fuller picture of your overall fitness trends. The key is choosing a scale that fits your lifestyle: consider which app ecosystem you already use (Apple Health, Fitbit, Garmin, etc.), the metrics you actually care about, and whether you want Wi‑Fi for automatic syncing or Bluetooth for occasional updates. Price ranges from very affordable to premium, but spending more doesn’t always guarantee better accuracy for weight alone. Focus on build quality, data consistency, and how easily the scale shares information with your other health tools. A scale that you actually step on every day is the one that helps you most, so pick a model that encourages consistency without unnecessary complexity.

Garmin Index S2 syncs directly with Garmin Connect, so athletes already invested in Garmin devices can see their weight and body composition alongside workout data and heart rate trends. The scale’s robust build handles daily use, and its data helps you adjust nutrition and training with precision. If you live in the Garmin ecosystem, this is the natural choice. Our pick: Garmin Index S2.

The RENPHO Bluetooth Body Fat Scale has a slim, lightweight design that slides under furniture or into a suitcase without adding bulk. It’s an ideal companion for dorm rooms, apartments, or anyone who moves frequently. Our pick: RENPHO Bluetooth Body Fat Scale.

Our verdict

No matter which model you choose, any of these smart scales will give you a clearer picture of your health trends.
